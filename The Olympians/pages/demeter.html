Demeter is revered as the goddess of agriculture, fertility, and the harvest. She's often depicted as a serene and motherly woman, carrying a bundle of wheat or barley in her arms.


According to legend, Demeter was the sister of Zeus and the mother of Persephone, who was kidnapped by Hades and taken to the underworld. Devastated by her daughter's disappearance, Demeter went on a quest to find her, and her grief and anger caused the earth to become barren.


Demeter is associated with the cycle of the seasons, and is often called upon to bless crops and ensure a bountiful harvest. She's known for her compassion towards mortals, and is often depicted as a kind and nurturing figure, who's willing to go to great lengths to protect and provide for her children.


But Demeter is also a powerful and fiercely protective goddess, who's not afraid to use her magic and influence to get what she wants. She's often portrayed as a formidable opponent to those who threaten her children or her domain, and her wrath can be devastating to mortals and gods alike.


Demeter embodies the virtues of fertility, nurturing, compassion, and fierce protectiveness.


Demeter's Roman name is Ceres.
