Hestia is revered as the goddess of the hearth, home, and family. She's often depicted as a modest and reserved woman, wearing a simple gown and carrying a kettle or a torch.


According to legend, Hestia was one of the original twelve Olympian gods and goddesses, and gave up her seat to Dionysus so she could tend to the sacred hearth of the gods. She's associated with the hearth and the sacred fire, and is often called upon to bless homes and ensure their warmth and security.


Hestia is known for her calm and gentle nature, and is often portrayed as a peaceful and nurturing figure, who provides comfort and stability to her worshippers. She's respected by mortals and gods alike for her purity and devotion, and is often invoked in prayers and rituals to bring harmony and balance to the home and the community.


But Hestia is not just a domestic goddess - she's also a powerful and independent figure, who values her autonomy and freedom above all else. She's often portrayed as a solitary figure, dwelling in her own domain, and refusing to take part in the conflicts and intrigues of the other gods and goddesses.


Hestia embodies the virtues of purity, devotion, nurturing, and independence. She provides a sense of warmth and security to the home and the community, and is revered for her calming and stabilizing influence.


Hestia's Roman name is Vesta.
