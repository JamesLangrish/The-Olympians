Hera is revered as the goddess of marriage, family, and childbirth. She's often depicted as a regal and imposing woman, wearing a crown and carrying a scepter or a lotus-tipped staff.


According to legend, Hera was born as the daughter of the Titans Cronus and Rhea, and later became the queen of the gods after marrying her brother Zeus. She's associated with the peacock and is often depicted with one by her side, symbolizing her beauty and power.


Hera is a proud and formidable goddess, who values loyalty, commitment, and tradition above all else. She's often called upon to bless marriages and protect families, and is known for her fierce protectiveness towards children and mothers.


But Hera is not just a domestic goddess - she's also a powerful and cunning politician, who's not afraid to use her wits and influence to get what she wants. She's often portrayed as a formidable opponent to Zeus, and is known for her intense jealousy and vindictiveness towards his many affairs which have been the subject of many Greek myths and legends.


Hera embodies the virtues of loyalty, commitment, tradition, and political savvy.


Hera's Roman name is Juno.
