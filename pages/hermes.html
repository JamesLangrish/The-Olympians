Hermes is the Greek god of commerce, communication, and travel. He's often depicted as a youthful and athletic figure, with a winged hat and sandals, and a staff called the Caduceus.


According to legend, Hermes was born as the son of Zeus and the nymph Maia. He's associated with cunning and intelligence, and is often called upon to help people in matters of commerce and communication.


Hermes is known for his speed and agility, and is revered as the god of messengers and travelers. He's also associated with luck and fortune, and is often invoked to bring good fortune to those who worship him.


But Hermes is not just a god of commerce and travel - he's also a patron of thieves and tricksters. He's revered as a master of deception and cunning, and is often called upon to help people in matters of stealth and subterfuge.


Hermes embodies the virtues of intelligence, speed, luck, and cunning. He's a powerful symbol of the human desire for commerce, communication, and travel, as well as the human capacity for deception and trickery.


Hermes Roman name is Mercury.
