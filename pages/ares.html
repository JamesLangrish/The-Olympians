Ares is the Greek god of war, violence, and bloodshed. He's often depicted as a muscular and aggressive figure, wearing armor and wielding a spear or sword.


According to legend, Ares was born as the son of Zeus and Hera, and is associated with the brutal and chaotic aspects of war. He's often called upon to bring destruction and mayhem to those who oppose him.


Ares is known for his fierce and unpredictable nature, and is revered as a god of courage and strength in battle. He's also associated with honor and valor, and is often invoked to inspire warriors and soldiers to fight with bravery and determination.


But Ares is not just a god of war and violence - he's also a patron of passion and desire. He's associated with erotic love and is often called upon to bring passion and excitement to people's romantic lives.


Ares embodies the virtues of courage, strength, and passion. He's a powerful symbol of the human desire for conquest and domination, as well as the human capacity for bravery and passion.


Ares Roman name is Mars.
