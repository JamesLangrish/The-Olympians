Poseidon is the Greek god of the sea, earthquakes, and horses. He's often depicted as a powerful and muscular figure, wielding a trident and wearing a crown made of seashells.


According to legend, Poseidon was born as the son of Cronus and Rhea, and is associated with the vast and unpredictable nature of the ocean.


Poseidon is known for his fierce and unpredictable nature, and is revered as a god of strength and power. He's also associated with horses and horsemanship, and is often invoked to bring success and fortune to those who race or ride horses.


But Poseidon is not just a god of the sea and horses - he's also a god of earthquakes and natural disasters. He's associated with the primal forces of the earth, and is often called upon to bring destruction and upheaval to those who oppose him. In this sense, he embodies the darker aspects of nature, and is a symbol of the human capacity for destruction and chaos.


Poseidon embodies the virtues of strength, power, and unpredictability. He's a powerful symbol of the human desire for adventure and exploration. And while he may be associated with destruction and upheaval, he's also a force of creation and renewal, reminding us of the ever-changing nature of the world we live in.


Poseidon's Roman name is Neptune.
