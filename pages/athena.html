Athena is a remarkable figure in Greek mythology, revered as the goddess of wisdom, strategy, courage, and inspiration. She's often depicted as a strong and intelligent woman, wearing a helmet and holding a spear and shield.


According to legend, Athena was born fully grown and armored from the head of her father Zeus, after he swallowed her mother Metis. She's associated with the owl and is often depicted with one perched on her shoulder, symbolizing her wisdom and foresight.


Athena is highly respected among the gods and mortals alike for her strategic mind and tactical skills. She's considered the patron goddess of heroes and warriors, and is often called upon for guidance and inspiration in times of war or conflict.


But Athena is not just a warrior goddess - she's also a skilled craftswoman and inventor, credited with inventing the plow, the trumpet, and the olive tree. She's associated with the city of Athens and is often depicted in art and literature as a powerful defender of the city and its people.


Overall, Athena is a multifaceted and fascinating goddess, embodying the virtues of wisdom, strategy, courage, and creativity.


Athena's Roman name is Minerva.
