Hades is known for his role as the ruler of the underworld. He is the eldest son of Cronus and Rhea, and was swallowed by his father along with his siblings. When Zeus overthrew Cronus, Hades and his brothers drew lots to divide the world among themselves, with Hades receiving the underworld.


Hades is often depicted as a somber and intimidating figure, dressed in dark robes and holding a staff. He is also sometimes shown wearing a helmet that makes him invisible, which he uses to move about undetected in the mortal world. Despite his fearsome reputation, Hades is not an evil god, but rather a stern and just one.


Hades' realm, the underworld, is the final resting place for the souls of the dead. Those who led good lives are sent to the Elysian Fields, a paradise reserved for heroes and the virtuous, while those who led wicked lives are punished in Tartarus. Hades is responsible for judging the souls and ensuring that the balance between life and death is maintained.


Hades is married to Persephone, daughter of Demeter. According to mythology, Hades abducted Persephone and made her his queen of the underworld. This event is said to have caused Demeter to go into mourning, resulting in the changing of the seasons. Hades and Persephone have a complicated relationship, but they are also a symbol of the cyclical nature of life and death.


Hades may be one of the more feared gods of Greek mythology, but he plays an important role in maintaining order and balance in the universe.


Hades Roman name is Pluto.
