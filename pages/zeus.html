Zeus is one of the most famous figures in Greek mythology, revered as the king of the gods and the god of lightning and thunder. He's often depicted as a powerful and regal figure, with a beard and a thunderbolt in his hand.


According to legend, Zeus was born as the son of the Titans Cronus and Rhea, and overthrew his father to become the ruler of the gods. He's associated with the sky and the natural forces of the world, and is often called upon to bring rain, lightning, and other forms of elemental power.


Zeus is known for his strength and power, but also for his cunning and his ability to shape-shift into different forms. He's also a notorious womanizer known for his many affairs with mortal and divine women, which often lead to conflicts and dramas among the gods and goddesses.


But Zeus is also a wise and just ruler, who values order and justice above all else. He's often called upon to arbitrate disputes among the gods and to protect the mortals who worship him. He's also known for his hospitality and generosity towards those who show him respect and honor.


Zeus embodies the virtues of strength, power, wisdom, justice, and hospitality. He's a dominant figure in the pantheon of gods and is revered as a symbol of authority and power.


Zeus's Roman name is Jupiter.
